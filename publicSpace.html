Racism In A Public Space

White supremacy and systemic racism are apparent in our parks and public places, where Black, Indigenous, and coloured people are subjected to suspicion, surveillance, harassment, violence, and death. Park People will not be able to fulfill its mission of “activating the power of parks to improve quality of life in cities” unless it acknowledges that systemic racism, oppression, and injustice are part of many coloured people's daily lived experiences in parks and public spaces. However, we must do more to address the issue that, in reality, parks are not for everyone due to racist gatekeeping mechanisms in public areas.

The Central Park birdwatching incident, which occurred little over a year ago (on May 25, 2020), is a well-known example of racism in a public area. In New York's Central Park, a conflict occurred between a white lady (Amy Cooper) strolling her dog and a black birdwatcher (Christian Cooper). When Christian requested Amy to put her dog on a leash in an area where one was needed, the conflict began. Amy began acting aggressively when she refused to put her dog on a leash. She threatened to contact the police later, claiming that "an African-American man is threatening my life." While Christian remained there recording Amy's phone conversation to the police, Amy's voice became increasingly frantic as the call progressed, despite the fact that Christian was standing still. Amy was eventually charged with filing a false report in the third degree. Had Christian not recorded this incident, he could've been put in jail for no reason or even worse. This is only one of countless instances of racism in a public setting.

The emotional toll of intergenerational trauma and estrangement is enormous, and it is not the job of minorities to educate people about history or how to be anti-racist. We are all complicit in the systems and behaviors, and we must face the emotional burden and the need to act together. Everyone in the urbanist community has to reconsider their responsibilities in this system and take steps to actively promote disadvantaged voices.
