Systemic Racism

Systemic racism is a complex topic which has many layers to it. It can be difficult to gauge the impact which systemic racism has on an individual's life or if they are contributing to it. The term systemic racism, also known as institutionalized racism, is used to describe the racial discrimination exhibitted by the social strcutures which make up our society. Whether it be laws which have the underlying aim of hindering a minority group in some way or even access to education based on the race of an individual. Systemic racism is much more prevalent than it may seem.

Abolishing systemic racism is one of the major difficulties in achieving modern day racial equality. It is often hard or even impossible for minority groups to reach the positions of power necessary to change the factors causing the systemic racism at hand. An example of this lies within the Peel District School Board of Ontario. It was found through a study that even though black students only made up 10.2% of the secondary school population they accounted for 22.5% of suspensions. This discrepancy in suspension rates led to a full investigation and report on the Peel District School Boards actions against anti-black racism as an organization.

Actions have recently been taken to tackle the immense and illusive issue of systemic racism. For example, in 2019 the Canadian government pledged 4.6 million dollars to establish a new Anti-Racism Secretariat within the Department of Canadian Heritage. Steps like these which implement the fundamentals of anti racism into our governments are key to stopping the continued plague of systemic racism.
